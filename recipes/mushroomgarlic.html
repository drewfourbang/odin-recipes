Mushroom Garlic Pork Chops

Tender Pork Chops smothered in a Garlicky butter and mushroom sauce

This simple but absolutely tasty recipe has only a few ingredients, but still creates a wonderfully savory profile.

The way the dijon mustard, garlic, and mushrooms all come together make this dish a classic in my household's diet.

Ingredients needed:

  • Boneless Pork Chops (Bone-in works as well)
  • Cooking Oil
  • Your favorite Seasonings (I use Chicken bouillone and Costco steak seasoning)
  • Your favorite Mushrooms
  • Dijon mustard
  • Minced Garlic
  • Ginger puree
  • Corn starch
  • Any broth

Steps to make the dish:

  1. Cook the pork chops thoroughly using your preferred method. I usually put them in an instapot and let it pressure cook on the “meat stew” pre-setting, which is around 28 minutes with about 6 pounds. I will drizzle some oil on the meat and sprinkle seasoning as well for flavor before closing the lid.

  2. Set the meat aside, and fill the pan with a good amount of butter or Oil. Put 3 or more spoonfuls of garlic, Along with about half a spoonful of ginger puree. Make sure to really put a ton of garlic in to add flavor, don't be shy.

  3. Chop up the mushrooms and dump all of it into the pot. Stir all until the mushrooms turn a crisp golden color.

  4. Add about 8-16 oz of dijon mustard along with your favorite seasonings. Stir to blend the ingredients together.

  5. To add to the amount of sauce, pour in your desired amount of broth. If the sauce turns into a more soup-like texture, then make a roux with the cornstarch to thicken it up. I usually use cornstarch mixed with broth or butter and microwave in a covered bowl until it turns into a mashed potato consistency. Mix into the sauce.

  6. Finally, combine the pork chops with the sauce. Make your favorite entree carbs to compliment the sauce, or eat straight like this if your particular about your diet like me!
